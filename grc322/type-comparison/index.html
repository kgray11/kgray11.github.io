Acumin vs. Aktiv Grotesk

History of each typeface

Aktiv Grotesk Typeface

The contemporary sans-serif typeface, Aktiv Grotesk, is designed by Swiss Typeface designer Bruno Maag and published by Dalton Maag in 2010. The intention behind the typeface was to design a modern, highly legible font compatible with a wide range of applications. With inspiration from classic grotesque typefaces, there are additionally subtle modern touches and incorporation of clean lines to suffice demand by contemporary design. Aktiv Grotesk features open apertures, a generous width, and a large x-height, making it exceptionally legible and accessible and suitable for both display and text use. Its large character set includes multiple styles, weights, and versatility for various design needs.

Acumin Typeface

Designed by Robert Slimach, the sans-serif typeface Acumin was released by Adobe Systems in 2015. It is another contemporary grotesque typeface family that offers versatility with its range of weights and styles. All the way from thin to black to condensed to extended. It was designed to be highly legible in multiple text sizes while maintaining elegance and clarity. Slimbach’s design process involved extensive research into classic grotesque typefaces, as he aimed to create a modern interpretation that would meet contemporary design desires. Acumin features a large x-height, balanced proportions, and open apertures that contribute to its excellent readability.

Comparison

Similarities

Acumin and Aktiv Grotesk are both grotesque typefaces, known for their neutral appearance, minimal stroke contrast, and simple geometric shapes. They offer a wide range of weights and styles, making them versatile and well-suited for various design purposes, while being optimized for excellent readability across different digital environments.

Differences

Acumin, designed by Robert Slimbach of Adobe, takes a more humanist approach with subtle details inspired by traditional serif typefaces, featuring slightly more open counters and a higher x-height compared to Aktiv Grotesk. In contrast, Aktiv Grotesk, designed by David Marshall and Ron Carpenter of Dalton Maag, adopts a more geometric and minimalist design approach, with fewer variations in stroke width and a narrower range of widths and styles.
